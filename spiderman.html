Spider-Man is one of the most beloved superheroes in the world. Created by writer Stan Lee and artist Steve Ditko, Spider-Man first appeared in Amazing Fantasy #15 in August 1962. Since then, he has become one of Marvel Comics’ most popular characters and has appeared in countless comic books, television shows, movies and video games.

At his core, Spider-Man is a relatable character. He’s not a billionaire like Tony Stark or a god like Thor; he’s just an ordinary guy trying to do the right thing. Peter Parker is a high school student who struggles with homework, bullies and girls just like any other teenager. But when he puts on his Spider-Man costume, he becomes something more. He becomes a hero.

Spider-Man’s powers are what make him unique. After being bitten by a radioactive spider, Peter Parker gained incredible strength, agility and reflexes. He can climb walls and shoot webs from his wrists thanks to his web-slinging devices. But it’s not just his powers that make him special; it’s his heart. Spider-Man is always willing to put himself in harm’s way to help others, even if it means risking his own life.

Over the years, Spider-Man has faced some of the most dangerous villains in the Marvel universe. From the Green Goblin to Doctor Octopus to Venom, Spider-Man has battled them all. But it’s not just his enemies that make him interesting; it’s his relationships with other characters. His friendship with Harry Osborn and romance with Mary Jane Watson are just as important as his battles with villains.

So whether you’re a longtime fan of Spider-Man or just discovering him for the first time, we hope you enjoy our website and all it has to offer!

Spider-Man is a superhero appearing in American comic books published by Marvel Comics. Created by writer-editor Stan Lee and artist Steve Ditko, Spider-Man first appeared in Amazing Fantasy #15 in August 1962. Since then, he has become one of Marvel Comics’ most popular characters and has appeared in countless comic books. Marvel has also published comic books featuring alternate versions of Spider-Man, including Spider-Man 2099, which features the adventures of Miguel O’Hara, the Spider-Man of the future; Ultimate Spider-Man, which features the adventures of a teenage Peter Parker in the alternate universe; and then Ultimate Comics: Spider-Man, which depicts a teenager named Miles Morales who takes up the mantle of Spider-Man after Ultimate Peter Parker’s apparent death.

Spider-Man has supplemented his powers with technology. Being a brilliant chemist and scientist, Peter has made web-slingers, bracelets that shoot out a sticky webbing, allowing him to swing from building to building and ensnare opponents. He has also developed stingers that shoot powerful energy blasts that can stun foes.

Spider-Man’s rogues gallery is one of the most iconic in all of comics. From the Green Goblin to Doctor Octopus to Venom to Carnage, Spider-Man has faced some of the most dangerous villains in the Marvel universe. But it’s not just his enemies that make him interesting; it’s his relationships with other characters. His friendship with Harry Osborn and romance with Mary Jane Watson are just as important as his battles with villains.

Spider-Man has also been adapted into numerous other media over the years. He has appeared in television shows such as Spider-Man: The Animated Series and The Spectacular Spider-Man; movies such as Sam Raimi’s Spider-Man trilogy and the Marvel Cinematic Universe’s Spider-Man films; and video games such as Spider-Man (2000) and Marvel’s Spider-Man (2018).
